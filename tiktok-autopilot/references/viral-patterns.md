## Hook Formula Library (English)

Hooks are the most critical element — first 1-2 lines of text on slide 1 or the first spoken sentence. They determine whether viewers swipe away.

### Category A: Relatability Hooks (Highest Engagement)
```
POV: you finally found something that actually works for [problem]
Me before [product]: [pain state] | Me after: [transformed state]
If you struggle with [pain], this is for you 👇
Nobody talks about how [problem] actually affects [outcome]
The moment I realized [common thing] was ruining my [result]
Tell me you have [problem] without telling me (I'll go first)
```

### Category B: Curiosity / Open Loop Hooks
```
I tried [product/method] for [X] days. Here's what happened 🧵
Stop scrolling — you need to know this if you [problem]
The [adjective] truth about [common solution/belief]
Why [popular thing] isn't working for you (it's not your fault)
What no one tells you about [niche topic]
I wish someone told me this before I [wasted time/money on X]
```

### Category C: Contrarian / Myth-Busting Hooks
```
[Common solution] is overrated. Here's what actually works:
Stop doing [wrong thing] if you want [desired result]
Unpopular opinion: [counterintuitive statement]
[Number] things [experts/influencers] won't tell you about [topic]
The reason [common advice] doesn't work (and what to do instead)
```

### Category D: Social Proof / Results Hooks
```
I [action] every day for [X days/weeks] and here's what changed
This [product/habit] went from [before metric] to [after metric] in [time]
[N] people tried this. Here's what actually happened
Why [type of person] swear by [solution] for [problem]
```

### Category E: List / Value Hooks (High Save Rate)
```
[N] signs you have [problem] (most people ignore #[X])
[N] things I wish I knew before [experience]
Rate yourself: how many of these [problem] signs do you have?
[N] [product category] that are actually worth it in [year]
```

## Slide Structure Templates

### Template 1: Pain Agitation → Solution (Best for Products)
```
Slide 1: [Hook — pain statement]
Slide 2: "Most people try [common wrong solution] but it fails because..."
Slide 3: "[Specific reason the pain persists]"
Slide 4: "[Product name] works differently. Here's why:"
Slide 5: "[Key USP / mechanism / benefit]"
Slide 6 (optional): "Try it → link in bio 🔗"
```

### Template 2: Transformation Journey
```
Slide 1: "I was [before state]. Here's how I changed everything."
Slide 2: "Month 1: [struggle/skepticism]"
Slide 3: "Month 2: [first signs of change]"
Slide 4: "Month 3: [result achieved]"
Slide 5: "What I used: [product] — link in bio"
```

### Template 3: Education → Product Reveal
```
Slide 1: [Surprising fact hook]
Slide 2: "Here's what actually causes [problem]:"
Slide 3: "[Science/mechanism explanation, simplified]"
Slide 4: "That's exactly why I started using [product]:"
Slide 5: "[How product addresses the root cause]"
Slide 6: "Get it here 👇 link in bio"
```

### Template 4: Comparison / Myth-Busting
```
Slide 1: "Stop wasting money on [expensive/ineffective option]"
Slide 2: "[Option A] vs [Product]: The honest breakdown"
Slide 3: "Price: $X vs $Y"
Slide 4: "Results: [negative] vs [positive]"
Slide 5: "Time to see results: [long] vs [short]"
Slide 6: "I switched [X] months ago and never looked back 👇"
```

### Template 5: List / Tips (Saves + Shares)
```
Slide 1: "[N] signs you need [product/solution] (save this)"
Slide 2: "Sign 1: [relatable symptom]"
Slide 3: "Sign 2: [relatable symptom]"
...
Slide N-1: "Sign [N]: [most surprising one]"
Slide N: "If you said yes to 3+, try [product] — link in bio"
```

## Caption Formula

```
[Hook line repeated or expanded]

[1-2 sentences adding context or urgency]

✨ [CTA: comment X, save this, link in bio, etc.]

[Hashtag block — 15-20 tags on new lines or separated by spaces]
```

**Caption best practices:**
- Keep the visible snippet (before "more") punchy — it's your secondary hook
- Always include a clear CTA
- Hashtags go at the bottom, never mid-caption
- Use 1-3 emojis max — don't overdo it

## CTA Library

**Soft CTAs (less friction, more engagement):**
- "Comment 'INFO' and I'll DM you the link"
- "Save this so you don't forget later"
- "Tag a friend who needs to see this"
- "Follow for more [niche] tips"

**Direct CTAs (product-forward):**
- "Link in bio to try it risk-free"
- "Use code [X] for [X]% off — link in bio"
- "Try it for [X] days → link in bio"

**Community CTAs (high comments):**
- "Tell me in the comments: have you tried [related thing]?"
- "Drop a 🙋 if this is you"
- "What's your experience with [problem]? 👇"

## What Makes Content Go Viral: Scoring Factors

When analyzing scraped posts in Phase 3, assess each for:

| Factor | High-Viral Signal |
|--------|------------------|
| Hook clarity | Viewer knows exactly what they'll get in 2 seconds |
| Emotional trigger | Frustration, hope, surprise, or humor |
| Relatability | "That's literally me" feeling |
| Curiosity gap | Can't stop reading without seeing the answer |
| Visual contrast | Clear text, readable font, bold colors |
| Pacing | New info on each slide, never boring |
| CTA friction | Low-friction ask (save, comment) beats "buy now" |
| Trust signal | Specificity, results with numbers, before/after |

**Minimum viable post:** Hook + 4-6 slides + clear CTA + 15 hashtags
